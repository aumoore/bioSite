Music Journey

Abel started his music journey off by releasing songs anonymously (original alias on Youtube was xoxxxoooxo) on Youtube for a while before meeting a producer named Jeremy Rose. Jeremy then helped produce three tracks with Abel; “What You Need”, “Loft Music”, and “The Party & The After Party” to which were abandoned by Jeremy to Abel under the condition that he would receive credit for them if they were ever successful. Abel posted two of these three tracks; “What You Need” and “Loft Music” to Youtube and eventually gained traction to which was acknowledged by rapper Drake. These then started getting coverage from multiple media outlets including Pitchfork and The New York Times. At this point he had settled on “The Weeknd” for his stage name. The removal of the ‘e’ was intentional so he could avoid any trademark issues with the Canadian pop rock band “the Weekend”. He also discussed how his stage name was chosen in tribute to the time he left school, took his mattress, and left one weekend and never came home.

Success & Achievements

The Weeknd has done a total of 7 music tours; they include The Fall Tour (2012), The Kiss Land Fall Tour (2013), King of the Fall Tour (2014), The Madness Fall Tour (2015), Starboy: Legend of the Fall Tour (2017), The Weeknd Asia Tour (2018), and After Hours til Dawn Tour (2022). He’s won 4 Grammy Awards, a Latin Grammy Award, 20 Billboard Music Awards, 6 American Music Awards, 3 MTV Video Music Awards, and 22 Juno Awards. He’s also received nominations for an Academy Award and a Primetime Emmy Award. In 2014, he was awarded the Allan Slaight Honour by Canada's Walk of Fame. The Toronto mayor also announced that the city would observe February 7th, 2021 as “The Weeknd Day” to commemorate Abel’s Super Bowl Halftime performance. The most popular song by The Weeknd is “Blinding Lights” which is the number 1 most streamed song on Spotify with 4.164 billion streams. With that, it was the first song to hold a spot in the top ten of Billboard's Hot 100 Songs for an entire year, and spent over 90 weeks on Billboard’s Hot 100 chart.
